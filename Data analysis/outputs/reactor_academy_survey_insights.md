# Reactor Academy Survey Analysis

Total usable responses: **107**.

## Core Finding
The survey supports Reactor Frontier Launchpad as a short, hybrid, practitioner-led sprint. Learners already know how to find information online, but they lack structured guidance, real industry access, hands-on work, and visible next steps.

## Key Numbers
- 89.7% are aged 18-24.
- 89.7% are university students.
- 93.5% start with YouTube, Google, or AI tools.
- Only 1.9% start with industry professionals or mentors.
- 47.7% say their current environment lacks real industry exposure.
- 72.0% prefer hybrid or in-person-heavy learning.
- 72.0% rate Reactor Frontier Launchpad 4 or 5 out of 5.
- Average Launchpad appeal score: 3.93 / 5.
- 57.9% say cost could stop them from joining.

## Strongest Design Priorities
- **Ecosystem-funded access model** (67.1/100): Make partners the main paying customer, not students.
- **Industry challenge sprint** (42.1/100): Anchor each cohort around one real partner challenge.
- **Build-to-portfolio prototype** (37.4/100): End with a visible artifact, demo, or prototype.
- **Career and opportunity bridge** (25.9/100): Include pathways, warm intros, and partner-facing demo day.
- **Beginner-friendly frontier on-ramp** (25.9/100): Start with clear foundations before specialization.

## Recommendation
Position Reactor Academy as an action bridge between learners and frontier industries. The launchpad should be partner-funded or heavily subsidised, built around real challenges, and designed to end with a portfolio artifact plus pathways into internships, research, startup ideas, or Reactor Ventures.
